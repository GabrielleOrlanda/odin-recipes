Sinigang na Isda sa Miso (Fish Stew with Miso)

Another classic Filipino dish of a soup that is often eaten with rice as a side.

Many variations exist for this recipe and its very delicious soup is something to come back to.

Ingridients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 colves garlic, crushed
  • 1 piece fresh ginger, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons miso paste, or more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauch
  • 5 cups water
  • 2 pounds red snapper fillets
  • 1 white radish, sliced
  • 1 bunch Chinese mustard greens with stalks
  • 3 green chile peppers
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi (Filipino lemon) juice, or more to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Cook adn stir onion, garlic, and ginger in hot oil for 5 minutes.
  2. Add tomatoes, cook adn stir until soft for about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in Miso paste and fish sauce then simmer until flavors are combined for about 2 minutes.
  4. Pour water into the pot and bring to a boil. Add snapper fillets and radish then simmer until radish softens, for about 2 minutes.
  5. Stir in mustard greens, chile peppers, and calamansi juice.
  6. Simmer, uncovered until greens are wilted for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  7. Transfer to a bowl to serve.
